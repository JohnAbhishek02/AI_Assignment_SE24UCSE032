
TURING TEST – DESIGN AND ARCHITECTURE

The Turing Test was proposed by Alan Turing in 1950 to determine whether a machine can demonstrate human-like intelligence.

Architecture Components:
1. Human Judge
2. Human Participant
3. AI System
4. Text Communication Interface

Working:
The human judge communicates with both the human participant and the AI system through a text interface.
The judge does not know which participant is the machine. If the judge cannot reliably distinguish
the machine from the human based on the responses, the machine is considered to have passed the Turing Test.

Purpose:
The goal of the Turing Test is to evaluate whether a machine can mimic intelligent human conversation.
